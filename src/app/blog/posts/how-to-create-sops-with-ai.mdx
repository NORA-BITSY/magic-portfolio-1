Processes that live in people's heads break when someone is busy, sick, or leaves. SOPs fix that — and AI can speed up the first draft.

## When to document with AI

- A task happens weekly with similar steps
- New team members ask the same questions
- Quality varies depending on who does the work
- You are about to automate a workflow

## Simple SOP workflow

1. **Record the rough process** — bullet points, voice note, or screen recording notes
2. **Run an SOP prompt** — ask for purpose, steps, inputs, outputs, and common mistakes
3. **Review and simplify** — remove jargon, add checkboxes, name owners
4. **Test with one person** — have someone else follow it once
5. **Store where work happens** — Notion, Google Docs, or your project tool

## Common mistakes

- Documenting ideal process instead of what actually happens
- Making SOPs too long to use under pressure
- Skipping the review step before publishing
